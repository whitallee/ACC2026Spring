Essential Course Information

The Blackboard learning management system

The primary organizational tool for this course is the Blackboard (Bb) learning management system. Navigation among the course elements is provided by the navigation bar that normally appears at the top of your screen or in a hamburger menu for smaller screens. The Bb system will be used for communicating with students, such as reporting grades, posting announcements, sending emails to individual students, and broadcasting emails to all students enrolled in the course.

The Bb Assignment and Test features are the mechanisms that you will use to submit your assignments and take your tests

The textbook

The primary textbooks for this course are three free eBooks that you can download by selecting the eBook links on the main Content page. (Select Content on the navigation bar.)

Students are also encouraged to make use of the free online resources shown below as supplemental learning material.

  • The Java Tutorials from Oracle
  • Introduction to Computer Science using Java by Bradley Kjell, Central Connecticut State University.

Quality Matters

This course, was designed according to the standards of The Quality Matters Higher Education Rubric Sixth Edition. As such, the course was designed around a hierarchy of properly aligned Course Level Objectives (CLOs), Module Level Objectives (MLOs), and Learning Activities (LAs). The alignment among these elements is shown in a course map that you can view by selecting the Course Map link on the main Content page.

The schedule

The general schedule of course activities progresses from the top to the bottom of the course map. The calendar schedule of activities can be viewed by selecting Gradebook on the navigation bar.

You can view a list of activities required from the start to the finish of the course by selecting Content/Start Here/Getting Started.

Objectives

The course is structured with three Course Level Objectives, about a dozen Module Level Objectives, and many Learning Activities.

The Learning Activities consist of readings from the eBook, tests keyed to the readings, and programming assignments keyed to the readings.

The tests and assignments can be accessed by selecting Content/LEARNING ACTIVITIES, selecting the relevant CLO, selecting the relevant MLO, and scrolling down and selecting the relevant test or assignment. They can also be accessed directly by selecting Content/Shortcuts and then selecting the relevant test or assignment.
