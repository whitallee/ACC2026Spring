Preface

DO NOT use the Blackboard Messages system to communicate with your instructor. That system is not monitored by your instructor and you will not get a response if you use it.

By design, this is an asynchronous section of the course. There are no scheduled interactions between the instructor and the students. The only scheduled events are the due dates for assignments and tests. If you need synchronous interaction with your instructor, you should probably transfer to a different section of the course.

Asynchronous interactions with your instructor primarily take place using email, phone, and video conferencing.

Email

Spam blocking software

I receive several hundred emails every day. Many contain viruses or worms. Most of the rest are SPAM.

Whenever you send a new email to me, you MUST compose and send the email from your ACC email account. Also, you must structure the Subject line of the email as follows:

Name, ITSE-2317-zzz, wwwww, Topic

Where:

  • Name is the name under which you are enrolled in the course (Sam Smith for example)
  • zzz is the section number of the course (002 for example)
  • wwwww is the five digit number assigned to your course (86074 for example)
  • Topic is the topic of the email

An example subject line follows:

Sam Smith, ITSE-2317-002, 86074, Question regarding Asg03

When I see that format, I will trust that the email is safe to open and read. Otherwise, I will assume that your email contains a virus or constitutes SPAM, and I will simply delete your email without reading it.

(Note that the format of the email that you are required to send following submission of an assignment is different. See that format elsewhere in this orientation material.)

Questions via Email

From time to time you may need to ask questions via email, and I encourage you to do so. However, please make the question as specific as possible. For example, "What is OOP?" is not a question that I can easily answer via email.

When appropriate, please illustrate your question with a short sample program. When you send a sample program, please observe several important rules:

  • Don't send isolated code fragments. Send a complete program that is ready for me to paste into a java file and attempt to compile.
  • Make the program as simple as possible while still illustrating your question. Eliminate all superfluous code that isn't necessary to illustrate the question.

II am here to help you, so don't hesitate to ask for help when you need it.

Response to Email

I normally respond to student emails within 24 hours except on weekends. If you send an email to me and you don't receive a response within 24 hours (allow 72 hours on weekends),, make certain that your Subject line is correctly formatted and send the email again.

Do not send executable attachments

If you send an email to me, DO NOT attach any executable files, even if they are encapsulated in a zip file. If your email contains executable attachments, the Gmail system will simply discard it and I will never see it. This includes files with the following extensions, just to name a few:

.exe
.com
.scr
.bat
.pif
.vb

The following file extensions should be OK:

.java
.class
.htm
.html
.css
.xml
.xsl
.doc
.zip (Provided the zip file doesn't contain any executable files.)

If you find it necessary to attach a questionable file for any reason, you should also send another email without an attachment notifying me that you sent the questionable file as an attachment so that I can be on the lookout for it.

Your official Email address

It is my understanding that all ACC students have been provided an official email address by ACC, and that address is reflected in all official ACC student records. I have been told that you can cause emails sent to that address to be forwarded to the email address of your choice. You should make it a point to check your ACC email, or your Bb emails on a daily basis.

When I need to send a broadcast email to all students in the course, I will normally send that email via Qwickly Email, which will automatically forward it to your official ACC email address.

When I need to send an email to you as an individual, I will send that email via ACC email to your official ACC email address.

If you prefer a face-to-face conversation, or you need to show me some code, I will be happy to join you in a video conference using either Google Meet during office hours. We can set that up using either email or phone.

Feedback on Assignments

You will have access to all assignments from the first day of class and you are encouraged to complete and submit them early. I normally grade your assignments within 24 hours after you submit them in Bb.

If you submit a correct solution to an assignment, I normally won't contact you. I will simply enter your grade in Bb. You can set notification features in Bb so that you will be automatically notified when I enter your grade.

If you submit an incorrect solution to an assignment, I will provide feedback in a manner that is described elsewhere in this orientation material. You will then be given an opportunity to correct your problems and submit another attempt.

Feedback on Tests

You will have access to all tests from the first day of class and you are encouraged to complete and submit them early.

Tests are graded automatically in Bb and you will receive feedback when you complete and submit a test. If you submit your first attempt early, you will be given another attempt to take the test provided that you can accomplish that before the deadline expires.

Discussion on any Course Topic

I am here to help you learn the material and successfully complete the course. Please don't hesitate to contact me on any topic related to the course.
