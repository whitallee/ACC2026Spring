"Attended" versus "Never Attended"

During the first few days of every semester, each instructor in every course at ACC is required to report whether each student has "Attended" or "Never Attended." the course.

Paraphrasing the requirement:

As of 09/21/16, attendance at an academically-related activity is defined by federal guidelines as:

1. Physical class attendance where there is direct interaction between instructor and student
2. Submission of an academic assignment
3. Examination, interactive tutorial or computer assisted instruction
4. Study group as assigned by school
5. Participation in online discussion about academic matters
6. Initiation of contact with instructor to ask question about academic subject

As of 09/21/16, selecting "Never Attended" will result in the student being automatically and administratively dropped from the course.

As of 09/21/16, a student may appeal the non-attendance drop and request a reinstatement in writing within five (5) business days from the date they are notified. The written appeal must be made to the course instructor, who must respond in writing within five (5) business days. The student may appeal the instructors decision in writing within five (5) business days to the appropriate Department Chair, then Instructional Dean, whose decision is final.

While I have the ability to override the administrative non-attendance drop and reinstate a student, I will do so only under the most compelling circumstances.

Please also be aware that if you are reinstated, you will still be required to adhere to the same schedule for all assignments and tests being adhered to by the other students in the course.

In this course, all students who fail to earn 100 points on orientation Test00 on or before the date shown on the Gradebook page will be reported to have "Never Attended", which will result in the student being automatically and administratively dropped from the course.

Completion of Orientation Test00 will be considered as having satisfied both Item 2 and Item 3 in the above list. Items 1, 4, 5, and 6 in the above list do not count as "Attended" in this course.

Prof. Baldwin
